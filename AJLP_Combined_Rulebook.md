
# AJLP Legal Citations Rulebook

This rulebook is based on OSCOLA but modified with key changes:
1. Source names (such as journal titles) must be written in full rather than abbreviated.
2. Abbreviations must include full stops in line with APA punctuation (e.g., p., pp., ed., vol., no.).
3. Access date for online sources are omitted.
4. URL can be included for all legal sources if available, but without the angle brackets.

The citation structure remains OSCOLA (footnote-based legal citation).

---
# PART 1 — CORE PRINCIPLES

- Use numbered footnotes.
- Provide full citation at first mention.
- Use shortened citation later.
- Case names and book titles remain italicised.
- Use full journal and report names rather than abbreviations.
- Use full stops in abbreviations such as:
  - editor = ed.
  - editors = eds.
  - volume = vol.
  - number = no.
  - page = p.
  - pages = pp.
  - paragraph = para.
  - part = pt.
  - article = art.
  - articles = arts.
  - section = s.
  - sections = ss.

Case Name [year] Law Report Series page (Court)

- Donoghue v. Stevenson [1932] Appeal Cases 562 (House of Lords).

- R v. Brown [1994] Appeal Cases 212 (House of Lords).

- R (Miller) v. Prime Minister [2019] United Kingdom Supreme Court 41.

Pinpoints:
- Donoghue v. Stevenson [1932] Appeal Cases 562 (House of Lords), p. 580.
- Carlill v. Carbolic Smoke Ball Co [1893] Queen’s Bench 256 (Court of Appeal), p. 270.

# APA Master Rulebook for References & In‑Text Citations (APA 7th ed.)

> **Scope**: This file consolidates *all* hard formatting rules, templates, examples, and edge cases extracted from the provided APA guides. Overlaps are merged into single definitive entries; university/course fluff is removed. Every source type is presented with a strict **Template / Example / Specific Rules** pattern. (Sources: apastyle.pdf; APA7-Style.pdf; apa-seventh-quick-guide.pdf; prior merged guide.)

---

## Global Rules

**Core mechanics**
- Use the **author–date** system for in‑text citations; provide a matching, alphabetized **References** list with **hanging indent** and **double spacing**. Heading **References** is **centered and bold**. citeturn3search2turn3search1
- **Punctuation & capitalization**: Periods separate elements; commas separate parts within an element. Titles of works (books, chapters, articles, webpages) use **sentence case** (capitalize first word of title/subtitle and proper nouns). **Journal/magazine/newspaper titles** use **title case** and are italicized; **journal volume** is italicized; **issue** in parentheses is not. citeturn3search2turn3search1
- **URLs & DOIs**: Present as active hyperlinks; format DOIs as `https://doi.org/xxxxx`; omit the label “DOI:” and omit “Retrieved from” unless a **retrieval date is required**. Do **not** end a DOI/URL with a period. citeturn3search2turn3search1
- **Authors in reference list**: List up to **20** authors; for **21+**, list first **19**, insert an **ellipsis** (no ampersand), then the final author. citeturn3search2turn3search1
- **Publisher location**: **Not included** for books/chapters. Omit “Co.”, “Inc.”; retain “Books” and “Press.” citeturn3search1
- **Same author & same year**: Differentiate with **a, b, c…** (order alphabetically by title); use suffixes in‑text as well. citeturn3search2
- **Edition/Report/Volume info**: Supply in parentheses **immediately after the title**; do not place a period between title and parenthetical info. citeturn3search1

**In‑text essentials**
- **Paraphrase**: Author (Year) or (Author, Year). Include page/para **only if required**; APA permits but many instructors prefer page numbers only for quotes. citeturn3search2
- **Direct quotes**: Provide locator: **p.** (single), **pp.** (multiple), **para.** (for unpaginated text), or **timestamp** (av). Block quotes **≥ 40 words**: new line, 0.5″ indent, double‑spaced, no quotation marks; citation after final period. citeturn3search2turn3search3
- **Two authors**: Always cite both; narrative uses **and**, parenthetical uses **&**. **Three+ authors**: Use **et al.** from first citation. citeturn3search2turn3search1
- **Group authors**: First citation—spell out name; abbreviate thereafter if recognizable (e.g., American Library Association **(ALA)** → later **ALA**). Keep abbreviation consistent. citeturn3search2
- **Unknown author**: Use **title** in place of author. Italicize standalone works; use quotation marks for articles. citeturn3search2
- **Multiple works in one set of parentheses**: Alphabetize; separate by semicolons. citeturn3search2
- **Secondary sources**: Use “**as cited in**” in‑text; include **only** the source you actually consulted in References. citeturn3search2
- **Personal communications** (e‑mails, interviews, DMs, classroom slides not publicly available): **In‑text only** with initials, surname, exact date; **no** reference entry. citeturn3search2turn3search1
- **Classical works**: Use standard numbering (act.scene.line; book.verse) instead of page numbers; show original year / version year (e.g., 1623/2003). citeturn3search2

# Books

## Authored Book (Print or E‑book; DOI optional)
**Template:**  
> Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). *Title of book* (Edition if not 1st). Publisher. https://doi.org/xxxxx (if available)

**Example:**  
> Fraser, C. (2017). *Prairie fires: The American dreams of Laura Ingalls Wilder*. Metropolitan Books. citeturn3search2

**Specific Rules:**
- Treat **e‑books** as print; include publisher; include **DOI** or **stable URL** if available; do **not** note format (Kindle, PDF, etc.). citeturn3search1

## Multiple Authors (1–20; 21+)
**Template:**  
> Up to 20 authors listed with initials; 21+: first 19 authors, …, Final Author. (Year). *Title*. Publisher.

**Example (21+):**  
> Kernis, M., Cornell, D., Sun, C., … Bach, J. (2012). *Cooperative endeavors in science education*. Random House. citeturn3search1

## Edited Book (Editors as Author element)
**Template:**  
> Editor, A. A., & Editor, B. B. (Eds.). (Year). *Title* (Edition). Publisher.

**Example:**  
> Melendy, R., & Kincaid, C. (Eds.). (2018). *Birth order and personality*. Doubleday. citeturn3search2

## Chapter in an Edited Book
**Template:**  
> Chapter Author, A. A. (Year). Chapter title. In A. A. Editor & B. B. Editor (Eds.), *Book title* (pp. xx–xx). Publisher.

**Examples:**  
> Gale, D. (2008). Innocence abroad. In L. F. Baum (Ed.), *The way home* (pp. 27–43). Cyclone Press. citeturn3search2  
> Store, W. (2003). The Doane ukulele method. In T. Miller & L. E. Davis (Eds.), *Music education in Canada* (4th ed., pp. 197–203). Vanwell. citeturn3search1

## Books with Group Author (and Publisher as Author)
**Template:**  
> Organization Name. (Year). *Title* (Edition). Organization Name.

**Example:**  
> Canadian Mental Health Association. (2007). *Mental health indicators for adolescents*. Canadian Mental Health Association. citeturn3search1

## No Author (Book)
**Template:**  
> *Title* (Edition). (Year). Publisher.

**Example:**  
> *Merriam‑Webster’s geographical dictionary* (3rd ed.). (1997). Merriam‑Webster. citeturn3search1

## Translated / Reprinted / Very Old Works
**Template:**  
> Author, A. A. (Year). *Title* (A. A. Translator, Trans.). Publisher. (Original work published Year)

**Example:**  
> Shakespeare, W. (2003). *Macbeth* (A. Smith, Trans.). Penguin. (Original work published 1623). *In‑text*: (Shakespeare, 1623/2003, 1.5.45–60). citeturn3search2

**Specific Rules:**
- Supply dual year in text `Original/Version` for classical/translated editions; close the reference with original year in parentheses. citeturn3search2

**Example:**

**Template:**  
> Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). *Title of webpage*. Site Name. URL

> Gomez, D. B. (2006). *A timeline of English literature*. URL citeturn3search1

## Webpage—No Author / No Date
**Template:**  
> Title. (n.d.). Site/Publisher. URL —or— Title. (n.d.). URL

**Templates:**  
> Author/Account. (Year, Month Day). Content up to 20 words [Description]. Platform. URL

# Special In‑Text Patterns & Edge Cases

- **Subsequent narrative citations in same paragraph**: You may omit the year after the first narrative mention (but **always** include year in parenthetical citations). citeturn3search1
- **Multiple citations in one set of parentheses**: Alphabetize and separate with semicolons; if same author, list years chronologically. citeturn3search1
- **Webpages without page numbers**: Provide the section heading and **paragraph number** (count down from the heading). citeturn3search1
- **Indigenous traditional knowledge**: If recoverable (video/audio/book/article), cite as that source type; if **not** recoverable, use **personal communication** pattern and adapted reference as appropriate to community guidance. citeturn3search1
- **Email/lectures/course slides (LMS-restricted)**: Treat as **personal communications** (in‑text only) unless your audience **has access** and a persistent URL exists. citeturn3search2
